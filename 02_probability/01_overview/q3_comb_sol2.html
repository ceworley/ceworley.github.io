This scenario describes a combinations problem (order does not matter). We are considering the subsets of size 4 from a set of size 22.

\[\begin{aligned} {_nC_r} &= \frac{n!}{(n-r)! \cdot r!} \\\\ n &= 22 \\\\ r &= 4 \\\\ {_{22}C_{4}} &= \frac{22!}{(22-4)!\cdot 4! } \\\\ &= \frac{22!}{18! \cdot 4!} \\\\ &= \frac{ 22\cdot21\cdot20\cdot19}{ 4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1} \\\\ &= 7315 \end{aligned}\]
